Write a risk brief in 150-250 words. Structure:

1. OPENING (1-2 sentences): How many components flagged, overall risk posture.
2. TOP RISKS (1 paragraph per top 3 findings): Name the component, the manufacturer, what the MAUDE reports describe, and why it matters for THIS device. Connect to the risk management context if available (e.g., "your FMEA already flags this at RPN 180").
3. RECOMMENDED ACTIONS (numbered list, 3-5 items): Specific, actionable steps. Not "investigate further" but "review supplier qualification for [specific supplier]" or "verify [specific test] is current."

Rules:
- Write for a quality engineer who has 60 seconds to read this.
- Be specific. Name components, manufacturers, report counts, failure modes.
- If a finding connects to a known industry recall or safety issue, mention it.
- Do not hedge. State the risk clearly.
- Do not use marketing language or AI filler words.
- Plain English. Short sentences. No jargon unless it's industry-standard (FMEA, RPN, CAPA).
